Analysis of World Happiness Report

The World Happiness Report is a publication. According to the respondents' evaluation of their lives, it contains happiness indexes and rankings of 140 countries . The report is also related to four (quality) life factors, namely, the contribution of GDP to the happiness index , the impact of life expectancy on happiness, the impact of national freedom on the happiness index, and the contribution of alcohol consumption to the happiness index. As of March 2022 , Finland has been rated as the happiest country in the world for five consecutive times.

World Map of Happiness

The world map shows the happiness scores of 140 countries from 2018 to 2022. The light green to dark blue in the figure shows the level of happiness index. The happiness index of developed countries is generally higher than that of developing countries.

It can be seen from the figure that Finland's happiness index keeps the first place all the year round (the happiness index is 7.63 to 7.82 ), while Afghanistan keeps the last place (the happiness index is 2.40 to 3.63 ).

World Rank of Happiness

The Bump Chart shows the top 10 happiest countries in the world. It shows that developed Switzerland has been hovering between 6th and 3th (7.48 to 7.57 ) for years, while for developed countries Denmark has been in second place at 7.60 until the turning point in 2019.

In addition, Sweden, a developed country, has the same trend as Denmark. In 2019, it entered a turning point and has always been ranked 7th with a score of 7.34 On the contrary, Finland, as a developed country, has always ranked first in the world with a score of 7.77 happiness index.

Contribution of alcohol consumption to happiness index

The radial chart shows the correlation between beer and wine consumption and happiness index in selected countries in 2016. From this figure, it can be seen that Germany's per capita beer consumption is 346 (happiness index is 6.99). On the contrary, Sweden's happiness index, which has only 152 beer consumption per capita, is 7.29. Therefore, the happiness index of countries with less beer consumption per capita is on the high side.

On the other hand, Switzerland's per capita wine consumption is 280 (happiness index is 7.51), while Iceland's per capita wine consumption is only 78 (happiness index is 7.50). Therefore, it can be concluded that the happiness index of countries with high per capita wine consumption is on the high side.

Contribution of life expectancy to happiness index

The scatter plot shows that the correlation between life expectancy and happiness index of countries in 10 regions of the world will be in 2021 . From this figure, it can be seen that Finland's life expectancy is 72 (happiness index is 7.84), while Afghanistan's life expectancy is 52.49 (happiness index is 2.52 ).

The trend of scatterplot is inclined upward. Therefore, it can be concluded that life expectancy is in direct proportion to the happiness index . The higher the life expectancy , the greater the happiness index .

Contribution of national freedom choice to happiness index

The heatmap shows the relationship between the choice of degrees of freedomand happiness index in selected countries from 2018 to 2022 . It can be seen from the figure that Australia has the highest degree of freedom from 2020 to 2021 (happiness index is 7.22 and 7.18 respectively).

However, most countries have the same changes with Australia . Therefore, it is concluded that the high degree of national free choice affects the change of happiness index. The high degree of free choice and the high happiness index.
